Join us for the 2026 DC Privacy Summit

The third annual DC Privacy Summit returns October 15, 2026, bringing together technologists, privacy advocates, and policy-minded professionals for fresh conversations about cryptographic privacy, illicit-finance risk, and responsible innovation.

Why

The DC Privacy Summit is a unique forum for urgent conversations about converging technological trends. Zero-knowledge proofs and other powerful cryptographic tools are raising novel questions about the law, and about the relationship between the US government and its citizens. Policymakers across the country (and around the world) are rushing to introduce digital identification credentials and age verification laws. Sophisticated cyberattackers are making consumer data security a national security issue. And the rise of AI is supercharging it all.

If favorable privacy, security, and policy outcomes are to be achieved, barriers to open communication between practitioners developing these new capabilities and policymakers in Washington must be overcome as soon as possible. This is the aim of the DC Privacy Summit event series. .

How

Imagine a pop-up think tank. In the morning we'll feature small, exclusive Chatham House Rule roundtable discussions that run concurrently and focus on specific topics including national security and crypto incident response, digital identity, and AI privacy and security.

Lunch will follow the conclusion of the roundtables.

The afternoon will feature a larger, on-the-record session with traditional conference talks, firesides, and panels. For a sense of how this will look and feel, please see videos of our previous live events, including the 2025 DC Privacy Summit and the Stablecoin Privacy Summit in April 2026.
